	\subsection{The brain of Vebot}. 
   We'll call our agent \textbf{Vebot}. It won't have a body, but rather a brain with data and relationships related to the events narrated in La Campa\~na Admirable. We'll build it using a programming language called Prolog, with the help of BlocklyProlog \footnote[1]{https://www.boles.de/programmierkurs-java/blocklyprolog/editor/index.html}.


    Vebot will be used as an active participant in a trivia game.

 Vebot's brain is composed of:

    \begin{itemize}
    	\item Constant concepts and ground relationships with just one argument. Facts are statements that are assumed to be true. In Prolog, facts are written using a predicate name followed by arguments. For example:
        \begin{lstlisting}
              comandante('Simon Bolivar').
                comandante('Jose Felix Rivas').
                comandante('Atanasio Girardot').
                comandante('Domingo Monteverde').
                comandante('Manuel Canas').
            	liberada('Merida').
                liberada('Barinas'). 
        \end{lstlisting}
	 

	\item Ground binary relationships: Relationships are characterized by having more than one argument that expresses the relationship between several objects. For example: jefe\_campana('Simon Bolivar', 'Campana Admirable'),  which indicates that Simón Bolivar is the chief of La Campa\~na Admirable. Another example is the relationship that indicates Simón Bolivar as the jefe of Atanasio Girardot and José Felix Rivas. It is indicated as:
        \begin{lstlisting}
           jefe('Simon Bolivar','Atanasio Girardot').
            jefe('Simon Bolivar','Jose Felix Rivas').
            jefe('Domingo Monteverde','Manuel Canas').  
        \end{lstlisting}

    \item  Relationships with more than two objects: Relationships are characterized by having more than two arguments that express the relationship between several objects. For example: The winning commander faces the losing commander in battle.
    
       \begin{lstlisting}
            /* Relationships between more than two: The winning commander faces the losing commander in battle.*/
            enfrenta('Atanasio Girardot', 'Manuel Canas', 'Agua de Obispo').
            enfrenta('Jose Felix Rivas', 'Jose Marti', 'Niquitao').
            enfrenta('Jose Felix Rivas', 'Francisco Oberto', 'Horcones').
            enfrenta('Simon Bolivar', 'Julian Izquierdo', 'Taguanes').
       \end{lstlisting}

        The causes of La Campa\~na Admirable, as well as the consequences, can be expressed as follows:      \begin{lstlisting}
          /* Causes of La Campana Admirable */
            causa('Perdida de la Primera Republica').
         /* Monteverde's threats of invasion of New Granada*/
            amenaza('Domingo Monteverde','Invasion a Nueva Granada').
            /* Desires of patriotic forces to restore independence*/
                deseos('restaurar la independencia').
        /* Consequences of La  Campana Admirable y */
            consecuencia('Decreto Guerra a Muerte', '15 de junio 1813').
         /*Loss of the royalist army of 6,000 troops*/
                perdida('ejercito realista', 6000).
        \end{lstlisting}
 
    \end{itemize}
    
    \paragraph{Questions to Vebot}
        Vebot's brain would be prepared to answer questions like the ones shown below.    
      \begin{itemize}
         \item Ground atomic queries: Is Simón Bolivar the leader of Atanasio Girardot? Is Monteverde the leader of José Felix Rivas?
        \begin{lstlisting}
             \'? jefe('Simon Bolivar','Atanasio Girardot').
	          \textbf{true}
	         \'? jefe('Domingo Monteverde','Jose Felix Rivas').
	          \textbf{false}
       \end{lstlisting}
	 \item Atomic queries with variables: Questions with an unknown to answer the question what, when, where? For this part, the concept of variable is introduced. It is a variable because its content can vary. Variables are denoted by a string consisting of letters, numbers, underscore characters, and beginning with an upper-case letter or underscore, Such as: X, Command, T66. 
         For example, the question what date did the Battle of Taguanes take place?
          \begin{lstlisting}
             '? batalla('Taguanes', X).   
                 '31 de julio 1813'
            \end{lstlisting}
        Another query using variable: Where the Battle of Niquitao takes place 
        \begin{lstlisting}
           '? lugar(X,'Niquitao').
        \end{lstlisting}
          \item  Conjunctive queries: queries using the logical conjunction operator, and. Eg, who is the leader of the patriotic army and leader of the Campa\~na Admirable?
            \begin{lstlisting}
               /* Leader of the Patriotic Army and Head of the Admirable Campaign */
                  '? jefe_campana(X, 'Campana Admirable'),lider(X,'Ejercito Patriota').
               /*   where the Decreto de Guerra a Muerte was signed */
                  '? consecuencia('Decreto Guerra a Muerte', '15 de junio 1813'),lugar(X,'Decreto Guerra a Muerte').
                   /* Routes to the Battle of Horcones site */
                  lugar(X,'Horcones'), ruta(X,Y).
           \end{lstlisting}
       \item  Rules: When the truth of a fact depends on the truth of another fact or a group of facts, a rule is used. Rules allow for more elaborate relationships between objects where concepts and consequences are declared. For example: 
       \begin{lstlisting}
         /* They are part of the patriotic army: they are commanders and their leader is Simon Bolivar. */
             efectivo_patriota(X):- comandante(X),jefe('Simon Bolivar',X).
         /* They are part of the royalist army: they are commanders and their chief is Domingo Monteverde.*/
             efectivo_realista(X):- comandante(X),jefe('Domingo Monteverde',X).
         /* Causes of  La Campana Admirable*/                      
         causa_campana(X,Y,W,Z):- causa(X), amenaza(Y,W), deseos(Z). 
     \end{lstlisting}
    \item Recursive rules: A recursive definition is one that is made in terms of itself. Recursion can be explained using the routes between cities:
       \begin{lstlisting}
            ruta(X,Y) :- conecta(X,Y).
            ruta(X,Z) :- conecta(X,Y), ruta(Y,Z).
        \end{lstlisting}
    \end{itemize}
 
\subsection{Ask Vebot}.
Ask Vebot is a question-and-answer game related to the events of La Campa\~na Admirable. 
The game consists of a deck of cards, some identified by a red stripe, and others by a blue stripe. 
Each card has the participant's question printed on its front side. Red cards indicate a true/false question. The blue cards, for their part, are questions whose answer requires the use of ground atomic queries, questions with variables or queries.
The layout of the deck of cards is shown in the figure \ref{fig:red}.

La gamificación se conceptualiza como la aplicación de elementos propios de los juegos en entornos ajenos al ámbito lúdico (Deterding et al., 2011), con el propósito de incentivar la participación y optimizar el rendimiento académico. Esta estrategia didáctica emerge como una alternativa para fomentar la motivación intrínseca y el compromiso en contextos educativos donde prevalecen la desmotivación estudiantil y la desconexión con los contenidos.
Es relevante señalar que las estrategias de gamificación en el ámbito educativo no se circunscriben a la mera incorporación de juegos o recompensas superficiales. Entre las estrategias de mayor eficacia documentada se encuentran:
 
Definición de objetivos concretos y alcanzables: Resulta fundamental para sostener el enfoque cognitivo y la motivación. Las recompensas pueden materializarse en diversas modalidades: sistemas de puntuación, medallas, insignias digitales, acceso a contenidos exclusivos o privilegios simbólicos.

Implementación de retos colectivos o individuales: Competencias de carácter formativo que estimulan la colaboración y la corresponsabilidad.
 
Provisión de retroalimentación inmediata: Facilita la corrección de errores in situ y la autorregulación del aprendizaje.

Diseño de niveles de complejidad progresiva: Genera en el discente una percepción tangible de avance y logro.

Personalización de las dinámicas de aprendizaje: Adaptación de actividades según perfiles estudiantiles, permitiendo elección de misiones, avatares o itinerarios formativos.
 
La  gamificación,  al  integrar  dinámicas  propias  del  juego  en  entornos  educativos,  representa  una 
estrategia  didáctica  con  alto  potencial  transformador.

The workshop is set for three academic hours:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Vbot goes to the school workshop: The first hour presents the objective of the workshop, the tool to build the Vbot knowledge base. To do this, the concepts of facts, objects, and relationships must be explained and how they are represented in the programming language.
    \item The Brain's Vebot: In the second hour, students must extract information from the provided text and encode it according to the provided programming language. You are also instructed on how to formulate ground atomic queries, atomic queries with variables, and conjunctive queries with variables and rules.
    \item Ask Vebot: The third hour is a question-and-answer game in which the student must ask Vebot a question that answers the questions posed. The questions are of two types: true-false, and queries involving variables.
\end{itemize}
